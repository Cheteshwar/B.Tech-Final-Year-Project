                                Incremental      commentary                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                    
                                Operating   expenses   growth  was   under  control at 7.3%  YoY   with Non-staff  expenses   flat  YoY at Rs       
                                67.9bn:  Staff  expenses  were  up  12.5%  YoY  to Rs 109.18bn,  driven  by provisions  for  employees,  which were 
                                up  32.0%  YoY  to  Rs  41.5bn  due  to higher  pension  provisioning,  owing to  decline  in  bond yields,  and  du
                                wage  revision impact.                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                    
                                Fee  income   growth  was  sluggish  at  4.0%  YoY  with  quantum growing  to Rs  51.77bn:  Loan  processing        
                                charges de-grew 3.7% YoY to Rs 6.17bn. Commission on LC/BG grew 18.9% YoY to Rs 7.04bn and                          
                                Remittance,  Collection,  etc  grew  38.2%  YoY  to Rs  16.06bn.  Miscellaneous  fee  income  de-grew  28.9% to  Rs 
                                6.78bn.                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                    
                                Comprehensive          Analyst    Meet   Takeaways                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                    
                                Asset  Quality                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    During the quarter, of the  total  provisions  made,  Rs. 23  bn  were towards  2  accounts.  These  accounts  
                                     are standard but are being treated as accounts in financial difficulty as per RBI’s June 7    th circular      
                                     framework.                                                                                                     
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Compared to what they guided for during the last quarter, the bank has increased its credit costs              
                                     guidance  by 40 bps to  1.4%  for  the full year.                                                              
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    The bank  stated that  its  credit  costs guidance  of  140  bps  includes the  potential haircuts  that  would
                                     to be taken on DHFL and a stressed renewable energy account. Combined standard provisions                      
                                     outstanding on DHFL   and  stressed renewable  energy  account are Rs. 23  bn. In  case  of the  renewable     
                                     energy account, fund based  outstanding  is  entirely  provided  for.                                          
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Agri slippages were  normal across  geographies  except  one state which  witnessed Rs.  20 bn worth  of       
                                     incremental slippages.                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    The bank stated that when an agri account gets classified as an NPA, other loans in the personal               
                                     segment  are also classified  as  NPA,  which  partly  explains  the  higher slippages  in  personal  segment  
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Post June  7th  circular  of  the  RBI,  the  Sashakth  program is  not  relevant  anymore,  as  stated  by  th
                                     Further,  based  on  this  circular,  the  bank  has  identified  46  accounts  which  are  eligible  for  sign
                                     which 6 NPAs   have  been admitted  to  the  NCLT  and  2 have  been  regularised.  Of the remaining  38, 18   
                                     are NPA  accounts  and  rest  20  accounts  are  standard  but maybe  SMA.  Of the  20 accounts  which  are    
                                     standard but maybe  in SMA,  2 accounts contribute 70%  in  value  terms  (Rs.  140 bn).                       
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Of the total  slippages  of Rs. 162.12  bn, there  is  one  account  which  contributed  Rs.  20  bn.  This  is
                                     account, which  is  being  serviced  regularly.  However,  out  of  the 18-19  lenders, only  one  bank could  
                                     complete  documentation   in  time  and  hence  this  account  had to  be classified  as  NPA. SBI  holds 90-  
                                     100%  provisions against this  account.                                                                        
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    The bank  expects resolution order  for  Essar  Steel  to  come through  this  month.                          
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Of the total  stock  of standard  accounts  where  ICA  has been  signed  or is  likely to  be  signed, which  
                                     worth Rs. 190  bn, one account  alone comprises  of Rs. 40 bn  which is in  the  renewable  energy  sector.    
                                     Another Rs. 50 bn  is  attributable  to  18  accounts.                                                         
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Last date for  the  resolution  of  Dewan  Housing  Finance  is  6th December  2019.                           
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    SME  slippages  during the quarter were higher compared   to last  year.  One  reason  is that  Q1FY19  had    
                                     RBI dispensation  in force for  exposure  worth  Rs.  13-15  bn,  which  was absent  in Q1FY20.  Secondly,     
                                     there have been  some  trading accounts  which slipped during the current quarter.  However some  of the       
                                     SME   slippages have  been  pulled back as  of  today.  Overall,  the bank  does  not expect  to  exceed  the  
                                     SME  slippages level seen last  year.                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    MUDRA    loans outstanding for  the  bank  is Rs. 260 bn. NPLs  in  this segment  are  Rs.  35 bn.

                                     Additionally,  out  of the Rs 190  bn worth  of  ICA exposure,  Rs. 140  bn  could slip  too  in  case resoluti

                                ?    Adjusting for the Rs. 19 bn  exceptional income from two accounts recovered      in Q1FY19, growth in          
                                     PPOP   on comparable  basis during the current  quarter  was  32%  yoy.                                        
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    On average  balance  basis,  loan  book  has  grown  by Rs. 265.63  bn  on  qoq  basis.                        
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    On the project financing  front  and  in terms  of  government  spending,  the  bank  is witnessing  a  number 
                                     proposals in the  oil  and  gas segment,  road  projects  and  renewable  energy projects.                     
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    In  the  personal  unsecured  loans  segment,  most  of  the  accounts  are  salaried accounts.                
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    The bank  sees the personal segment   (retail)  growing  at 18-20%.                                            
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Most  of the retail  loans  are  from  people  who are  account  holders  in  SBI. 90-95%  of  the  home loan  
                                     customers  are salaried  and  a significant  chunk  is  government  employees.                                 
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    On core basis, the bank is  looking  at an ROA  of 50-60  bps  for FY20.                                       
                                                                                                                                                    
                                ?    Credit  growth  guidance  for  FY20  is  12%.
